Guided Navigation in University of Washington Medical Center. (Web App)

This app was made to help people trying to find their way through the labyrinth of the UW Medical Center. It uses an on-screen directional arrow to guide the visitor toward the right intersections, eventually leading to the entrance of Integrated Brain Imaging Center.

The app uses geolocation to detect one of the 4 entrances, calibrates the compass direction (hint: magnetic interference indoors, no GPS), and in some paths offers choices for using stairs or elevators.

HTML, JavaScript, CSS; TextWrangler, CSSEdit, Web Inspector, bash

Gaze Tracking Interface.

This was developed as my graduation project at MPEI-Festo. I was already familiar with basic Computer Vision, so I took around 6 weeks to decide on the algorithm and implement it. The app used a laptop webcam to detect the user's gaze direction, then move the mouse pointer a set distance in the corresponding direction.

I could left or right click by holding either of my eyes closed. Scrolling was done by looking up or down with the left eye while having the right eye shut.

MATLAB CLI + GUI IDE
